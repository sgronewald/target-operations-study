# A Case Study on Target’s E-Commerce Operations in Brazil


## Executive Summary:
This project analyzes the operational performance and growth trajectory of Target in the Brazilian market. By evaluating approximately 100,000 orders spanning a 25-month period, this study identifies a core paradox: while customer satisfaction is high due to frequent early deliveries, a conservative logistics algorithm may be inadvertently stifling conversion rates. This analysis offers a strategic roadmap for geographic expansion into the North/Northeast regions and suggests an inventory pivot toward high-end goods to leverage Brazil’s installment-based payment culture.

## Market Performance and Growth Analysis
* **Operational Timeline:** The dataset covers operations from September 4, 2016, to October 17, 2018.
* **Revenue Scaling:** Target successfully transitioned from a small-scale business in late 2016 to a mature e-commerce entity, maintaining a consistent monthly revenue of R$ 1 million by 2018.
* **Order Velocity:** Monthly order volume saw a drastic increase, rising from approximately 300 orders in late 2016 to over 6,000 orders by the end of the 2018.
* **Growth Lifecycle:** The platform experienced a massive 705% Year-over-Year (YoY) surge in order volume between January 2017 and January 2018. Following the initial surge, growth entered a stabilization phase, normalizing to a sustainable 51% YoY increase by August 2018.
* **Seasonality:** Sales are heavily event-driven, with November emerging as the peak performance month. This is primarily attributed to Black Friday, which serves as a major catalyst for e-commerce in Brazil.
* **Peak Transaction Windows:** Daily order activity reaches its zenith during the afternoon between 13:00 and 18:00, indicating high consumer engagement during standard business hours.
* **Geographic Distribution:** Demand is highly concentrated in the South East region, specifically within the urban hubs of São Paulo (SP) and Rio de Janeiro (RJ). This highlights a strong regional footprint and a significant opportunity for future national expansion.

## Logistics and Delivery:
* **Geographic Barriers and Delivery Costs:** Operations are heavily centralized in the South and South-East, where the vast majority of sellers are located. This concentration results in high freight costs and extended delivery times for the North, North-East, and Center-West. Specifically, deliveries to Northern states are complicated by the Amazon rainforest, leading to average transit times exceeding 20 days.
* **Urban vs. Remote Areas:** Urban states drive the highest order volume and total revenue but maintain the lowest average order value and freight costs in the country due to established infrastructure. On the contrary, remote states exhibit low order volume but significantly higher average order values and freight costs—averaging 2.8x higher than urban areas—suggesting that customers bundle high-value items to offset shipping premiums.
* **Strategic Expectation Management:** Target employs highly conservative delivery estimates nationwide. This gap between expectation and reality is most pronounced in remote states. The overly conservative promised arrival date is an expectation management strategy that leads to higher customer satisfaction scores. The data reveals a strong correlation between early delivery and satisfaction ratings, with early deliveries leading to 80% positive reviews and late deliveries leading to 54% poor reviews.
* **Conversion Trade-off:** While the expectation management strategy protects brand reputation and secures positive reviews, the inflated delivery timelines likely act as a deterrent, discouraging potential customers from completing purchases during the checkout phase.

## Payments and Pricing: 
* **Credit Dominance:** Credit cards are the primary payment method which accounts for 75% of all fulfilled orders
* **Installment Culture:** Brazilian consumers show a strong preference for interest-free, long-term installments.
* **Inventory Concentration:** Currently, 95% of Target’s inventory consists of items priced under R$500. This indicates a strong focus on affordable, mid-range products.
* **High-End Opportunity:** Analysis reveals a direct link between installment options and purchasing power. At the 24-installment level, the average order value surges by 160%. This suggests that when long-term installments are available, customers use them to buy premium products rather than just paying off cheaper items slowly.
* **Inventory Gap:** Expensive, high-end products are currently underrepresented in the inventory. There is a clear opportunity to increase revenue by expanding the premium product range to match the consumer's willingness to use long-term credit for larger purchases.

## Actionable Insights and Recommendations: 
* **Afternoon Flash Sales:** Introduce time-sensitive discounts between 16:00 and 18:00 to capitalize on peak daily traffic and convert browsing window-shoppers into buyers.
* **Event-Driven Marketing:** Launch targeted advertising campaigns well before major Brazilian holidays (Black Friday, Christmas etc.,) to capture early consumer interest.
* **Northern Hub Expansion:** Establish a warehouse in Bahia (BA). Outside the South-East hub, BA has the strongest existing customer base and acts as a geographic bridge. This would drastically reduce delivery times and freight costs for the North, North-East, and Center-West regions.
* **Optimized Delivery Estimates:** Adjust the logistics algorithm to provide more realistic, tighter delivery windows. This can decrease cart abandonment without sacrificing customer satisfaction.
* **Regional Seller Onboarding:** Launch incentive programs such as reduced commission rates for the first 6 months to attract sellers in the North, North East and Center-West.
* **Tiered Free Shipping:** Implement free shipping for remote areas above specific order thresholds. Offset these costs by leveraging high order volumes to negotiate lower fees with South-East carriers.
* **High-End Inventory Pivot:** Increase the stock of premium products (R$ 500+) to better utilize the Brazilian consumer's preference for long-term, interest-free credit installments.
